Potato

The potato is a starchy food native to the Americas and is a perennial in the nightshade family Solanaceae. It was originally believed to have been domesticated by Native Americans in multiple locations, but genetic studies traced a single origin to present-day southern Peru and extreme northwestern Bolivia. It was introduced to Europe by the Spanish in the 16th century and is now a staple food in many parts of the world. It contains the toxin solanine, which is dangerous for human consumption. Green sections of the plant can accumulate a high concentration of glycoalkaloids, which can affect human health.

Cheese

Cheese is a dairy product produced in a variety of flavors, textures, and forms by coagulation of the milk protein casein. It is valued for its portability, long shelf life, and high content of fat, protein, calcium, and phosphorus.
